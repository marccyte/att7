Overall Retrospection

Looking back on my practice teaching journey, I can honestly say it has been one of the most transformative experiences in my life. What started as a requirement for graduation quickly became a deeply meaningful chapter—one that challenged me, inspired me, and ultimately helped me grow into the kind of teacher I’ve always aspired to be.

Teaching at General Emilio Aguinaldo National High School (GEANHS) opened my eyes to the real landscape of the classroom. Beyond the lesson plans and teaching demonstrations, I discovered the heart of education: connection. I learned that being an effective educator isn't just about delivering content, but about reaching students where they are—emotionally, mentally, and intellectually.

I was given the opportunity to teach Grade 7 students, handling different sections that brought their own unique energy and learning styles. Under the supervision of Ma'am Mary Carlyle M. Pineda , I handled Sections Rose, Ilang-Ilang, and Lily—each filled with bright young minds eager to learn. I also had the privilege of teaching SMART-1, SMART-2, and the STE class under the guidance of Sir Marlon T. Silva. Every classroom experience taught me something new, not just about teaching, but about patience, flexibility, and the beauty of learning alongside my students.

Throughout this journey, I encountered both struggles and successes. There were days filled with self-doubt, when lessons didn’t go as planned or students seemed unengaged. But there were also moments of genuine joy—when a student finally grasped a difficult concept, when classroom laughter echoed after a well-placed joke, and when I received feedback that reminded me of my purpose.

I am incredibly grateful to my coordinating teachers, Ma’am Mary Carlyle M. Pineda and Sir Marlon T. Silva, whose mentorship was both constant and compassionate. They offered not just technical advice but moral support during moments of uncertainty. I also thank Ma’am Elizabeth Z. Villanueva, our head teacher, for her firm yet encouraging presence, and Ma’am Lerma V. Peña, the school principal, for welcoming us into GEANHS with warmth and trust.

A special thank you goes to Prof. Steve Anapi, my field study supervisor, for guiding not just my teaching practice, but also my personal and professional development. His mentorship, grounded in experience and wisdom, helped me reflect more deeply on my identity as a future educator.

This experience taught me that teaching is not just a profession—it is a calling. It requires patience, empathy, creativity, and an unshakable belief in the potential of every learner. As I close this chapter of my practice teaching, I do so with a full heart, carrying the stories, lessons, and memories of every student I encountered. I now step forward, not as a perfect teacher, but as one committed to lifelong learning—ready to teach, ready to inspire, and ready to grow.
