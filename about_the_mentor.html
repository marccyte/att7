About My Mentors

These are the mentors who played a significant role in my practice teaching journey. Their guidance, support, and encouragement have been incredibly valuable in shaping me into the kind of teacher I aspire to be. I am truly grateful for everything they have imparted—both in terms of practical classroom strategies and in building the mindset of an effective and compassionate educator.

My coordinating teachers, Ma'am Mary Carlyle M. Pineda and Sir Marlon T. Silva, were with me every step of the way during my practice teaching at General Emilio Aguinaldo National High School (GEANHS). They provided consistent mentorship, shared their professional insights, and helped me navigate the real challenges of the teaching profession. Their dedication to guiding student teachers like me made a huge difference in my growth.

I also want to acknowledge the Head Teacher, Ma'am Elizabeth Z. Villanueva, for her leadership and openness to welcoming us into the GEANHS community. Her presence provided a solid foundation of support within the department.

Moreover, my sincere appreciation goes to my field study supervisor, Prof. Steve Anapi, who oversaw not only our journey at GEANHS but also in other coordinating schools. His commitment to mentoring us and ensuring we maximize our learning experience speaks volumes of his dedication to future educators.

Lastly, I would like to express my gratitude to the Principal of GEANHS, Ma'am Lerma V. Peña, for allowing us to become part of their academic environment and for fostering a school culture that supports learning, growth, and collaboration.

Each of them contributed uniquely to my journey, and I carry the lessons and memories from this experience with great pride and gratitude.
