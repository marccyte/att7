The First Week: Adjusting To A New Role

During our first week of deployment, I felt an overwhelming pressure. It was our first time in a real teaching environment — no longer as students, but as actual teachers. I was assigned to Grade 7, but I didn’t get much interaction with the students initially since I was mostly inside the faculty room. Transition to Higher Sections

A week later, I was assigned to SMART 1, SMART 2, and the STE class — all higher sections. Handling them came naturally, as most students were focused and disciplined. A simple reminder was enough for them to listen and stay on task.

Teaching them was enjoyable, especially with the mentorship of Sir Marlon, my other cooperating teacher. He consistently provided feedback and suggestions, which helped me improve. We built a great professional relationship, making my PT journey smoother.

“Boss” – A Nickname I Didn’t Expect

The students playfully called me “Boss” because of how I dressed and taught. While I appreciated the nickname, I reminded them to call me “Sir” or “Teacher Marc” in school, as a sign of respect.



Bittersweet Goodbye to Higher Sections

Eventually, I had to leave the higher sections to start handling Ma’am Pineda’s classes. It was bittersweet. We had built a connection, even if only for two weeks, but I knew they had learned much and genuinely enjoyed our lessons.

Grade 7: Challenges and Growth

I then transitioned to teaching Grade 7 – Rose, Ilang-Ilang, and Lily. It was a challenge, but I saw a reflection of my younger self in them. Despite their energy and noise, they gave me a warm welcome. Through them, I learned what it truly means to be a teacher, especially how to be patient.

Thanks to the instructional materials I prepared, they enjoyed our lessons because I designed them to feel more like play than work. Even as we had fun, I always ensured they were learning.

There was never a dull moment in class. I was especially proud when they all passed their exams — proof that every sleepless night and effort was worth it.

Reflections and Farewells

Every lesson plan I made and every move I executed — carefully checked by Ma’am Pineda — felt worth it. Like my earlier experience with the higher sections, saying goodbye to my Grade 7 students was the hardest part.

As we wrapped up our journey, we bid farewell to Ma’am Beth, our Math Department Head, and Ma’am Lerma, the school principal. Of course, I’ll always be grateful to my beloved CTs — Ma’am Pineda and Sir Marlon — who helped shape me into the teacher I am today.

Before leaving, I gave them a parting gift — a little token, “The Real Boss,” as my students called me, but in truth, they were the real inspiration behind this unforgettable journey.
