About The Teacher

Hi! I'm Marc Lorens R. Fernandez — a passionate learner, creator, and future educator, born on November 23, 2001, and proudly raised in Tanza, Cavite.

My love for Mathematics started in Grade 2, and ever since then, solving math problems has felt like solving mini-puzzles — exciting, challenging, and deeply rewarding. I discovered my passion for teaching back in Grade 12, thanks to the guidance and inspiration of my adviser, Sir Dom, who sparked my interest in making learning meaningful for others.

Outside the classroom, I dive deep into programming, especially web development and robotics (working with Arduino and Atmel). I also enjoy drawing, creating music and videos, and expressing myself through various art forms. For over four years, I’ve been the main vocalist of a band, performing at gigs and open mics just for the love of it.

Oh, and my age? You do the math — HAHAHA just kidding, I’m already 23.

Whether it’s through teaching, tech, or creativity, I’m all about exploring, building, and inspiring. Let’s keep learning together!

Teaching Philosophy

Basic? Basic!

That’s my classroom tagline—because I believe no lesson should feel intimidating or out of reach. My name is Teacher Marc, and I teach with one core principle: complex ideas become simple when broken down with clarity, creativity, and a little bit of fun. I specialize in transforming so-called "hard" topics into engaging, digestible lessons—because when students realize they can grasp concepts they once feared, their confidence soars.

In my classroom, we ditch the stress and embrace the "Aha!" moments. Through relatable examples, interactive problem-solving, and a relaxed (but never lazy) approach, I help students see that every challenge is just a series of basic steps stacked together. My goal isn’t just comprehension—it’s mastery without the anxiety. Because when learning feels accessible, students stop worrying about failing and start enjoying the process.

So yes—"Basic? Basic!" means no smoke and mirrors, no overcomplicating, just solid understanding. And if a student ever says, "Wait, that’s it?" — I know I’ve done my job.
